A Community Project For Safer Cycling

The Close Call Database for Cyclists has been set up to connect cyclists together to share important information about the dangers that we are facing. "Share the Road" is a nice idea -- in fact, law -- but in practice too many of us are being needlessly injured, maimed, and killed. This site allows cyclists to contribute and collect critically important data for use by cycling advocates, law enforcement, law makers, and the medical community who treat our injuries.

Hostile Drivers

Identifying hostile drivers that intentionally harass cyclists is a primary focus of this site. The goal is to identify them, warn other cyclists in your area, and bring them to the attention of local police.

Collisions

Gather high quality, high integrity data from the cyclist's perspective that enables us to learn as much as possible from the accidents that occur, allowing us to make data-driven conclusions and seek data-driven solutions.

Dangerous Locations

An effort to map high risk intersections, roadways and other areas where accidents frequently occur. These areas may exhibit a large number of "close calls" and are probably candidates for infrastructure improvement.

Crime & Punishment

An effort to follow criminal and civil proceedings from collision to conclusion so that we can identify and attempt to address holes in the legal system that exist in some jurisdictions.

We also need to be accountable for the way that we share the road.   So please ride responsibly!

About This Project

The idea for this site is not a new one. Hundreds if not thousands of cyclists have wished for a site like this. The only thing new is that one cyclist with technology skills got buzzed by one car too many and decided to do something about it.

What can you do to help? The first and most important thing is to register. This is a cyclist-to-cyclist notification system. All networks grow more powerful as people join. We become each others' eyes and ears. Because the site will notify you about problems in your area, it's also in your interest to have a large number of riders in your area register. So don't just sign up. Sign up and tell your friends to do so as well.

You can register using Strava or create a custom account.
